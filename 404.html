The Platform

How Popson Works

A verified, escrow-backed, lawyer-reviewed, police-linked real estate platform. Here's every layer of protection we've built.

🪪

Layer 1 — Identity Verification (Both Parties)

Every property owner and every renter/buyer must verify their identity before any transaction begins. Verification is a 3-step manual process: upload a government-issued photo ID (National ID, Driver's Licence, Voter's Card, or International Passport), submit a live selfie for face comparison, and confirm your phone number via OTP. A next-of-kin declaration adds a third layer of accountability. Our internal team reviews every submission within 24 hours. NIMC API integration is planned for a future phase and will add automated verification without changing anything users see.

📄

Layer 2 — Property Title Verification

Before a listing goes live, our internal team — supported by a panel of NBA-registered lawyers — verifies the property's title documents against state land registry records. We check for encumbrances, government acquisition zones, ownership disputes, and document authenticity. Only properties that pass this review appear on the platform.

⚖️

Layer 3 — Lawyer Panel

All buy transactions and commercial leases include a review by a Popson-panel lawyer registered with the Nigerian Bar Association (NBA). The lawyer reviews title documents, drafts or reviews the transaction agreement, and certifies the deal before funds are released from escrow. Legal fees are charged separately from the platform fee.

🔒

Layer 4 — Escrow-Protected Payments

All transaction funds are held in a CBN-compliant escrow account — ring-fenced, never commingled with Popson's operational funds. Funds are released only when both parties confirm handover on the platform. 90% goes to the property owner; 10% is Popson's platform fee. If a listing is found fraudulent after payment, the full amount is refunded.

👮

Layer 5 — Nigeria Police Force CID Liaison

Popson Geospatial Services maintains a formal MOU with the Nigeria Police Force Criminal Investigation Department (CID). When a fraud complaint is raised, the platform automatically generates a case file containing the verified identities of both parties, the property records, and the full transaction trail — and submits it to the NPF liaison. Victims no longer need to "prove" fraud from scratch.

📍

Layer 6 — Geospatial Intelligence

Every property is pinned to a precise GPS coordinate. Listing photos must be geo-tagged and taken at the property location — we validate EXIF coordinates against the declared address (within 200m). The interactive map lets you search by state, LGA, or draw a custom area. Future layers will add proximity intelligence: schools, hospitals, police stations, and infrastructure.

Privacy Policy

Effective date: 7 June 2026 · Popson Geospatial Services · In compliance with the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA)

1. Who We Are

GeoEstate is a geospatial real estate marketplace operated by Popson Geospatial Services. Our platform connects verified property owners with verified renters and buyers across Nigeria. We are committed to protecting your personal data in compliance with the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA) and the Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR).

Contact: Popoola Idris Bamigboye · popson0205@gmail.com · +234 916 042 0100

2. What Data We Collect

We collect the following categories of personal data when you register or use our platform:

Identity data: Full name, date of birth, gender, government-issued ID (National ID Card, Driver's Licence, Voter's Card, or Passport)
Contact data: Phone number, email address, residential address
Biometric data: Live selfie photograph (used only for manual face comparison against your ID document)
Financial data: Bank account details for receiving payments (property owners only); transaction reference numbers
Property data: Property address, title documents, geo-tagged photographs
Next of kin data: Name, phone number, relationship
Usage data: Pages visited, search queries, device type (collected anonymously)

We do not collect or store raw National Identification Numbers (NIN). Identity verification is conducted manually by our team using uploaded documents.

3. Why We Collect Your Data

We collect and process your personal data for the following lawful purposes:

✅ Identity verification — to confirm you are who you say you are before allowing property listings or transactions
✅ Fraud prevention — to detect and prevent fraudulent listings, impersonation, and illegal fee demands
✅ Transaction processing — to facilitate payments, generate agreements, and coordinate handovers
✅ Legal compliance — to comply with Nigerian law, including the Money Laundering (Prevention and Prohibition) Act 2022 and the NDPA 2023
✅ Law enforcement cooperation — to provide verified identity records to the Nigeria Police Force CID when a fraud complaint is filed, as disclosed in our consent agreement
✅ Platform communications — to send you verification updates, payment confirmations, and transaction alerts

4. How Long We Keep Your Data

We retain your personal data for as long as your account is active. If you close your account, we retain your data for 7 years in compliance with Nigerian financial record-keeping requirements. Biometric selfie data (face photographs) is retained for 2 years from the date of submission, after which it is deleted. Transaction records are retained for 7 years.

5. Who We Share Your Data With

Other verified platform users: Your verified badge and first name are visible to other verified users when you initiate a transaction. Your full contact details are only shared after both parties confirm interest.
GeoEstate lawyer panel: NBA-registered lawyers assigned to your transaction receive your verified identity and property documents as required to process the transaction.
Nigeria Police Force CID: In the event of a fraud complaint or dispute, your verified identity records and transaction trail are shared with the NPF Criminal Investigation Department. You consent to this when signing the platform consent agreement.
Payment processors: When Paystack is integrated (Phase 2), your bank account details are shared with Paystack solely for the purpose of processing transactions.

We do not sell your personal data to third parties. We do not share your data with advertisers.

6. Your Rights Under the NDPA 2023

As a data subject under the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023, you have the following rights:

📋 Right to access — request a copy of all personal data we hold about you
✏️ Right to rectification — request correction of inaccurate data
🗑️ Right to erasure — request deletion of your data (subject to legal retention requirements)
🚫 Right to object — object to processing of your data for specific purposes
📦 Right to portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
⏸️ Right to restriction — request that we limit processing of your data in certain circumstances

To exercise any of these rights, email popson0205@gmail.com with the subject line "Data Subject Request." We will respond within 30 days.

7. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. All data is transmitted over HTTPS. Document uploads are stored securely. Access to personal data is restricted to authorised GeoEstate staff on a need-to-know basis.

8. Cookies

GeoEstate uses only functional cookies — specifically localStorage — to remember your theme preference (light/dark mode) and session state. We do not use advertising cookies or third-party tracking cookies.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy as our platform evolves or as Nigerian law changes. We will notify registered users of material changes by email or SMS at least 14 days before the change takes effect. The current version is always available at this page.

10. Complaints

If you believe we have handled your personal data in a manner that violates the NDPA 2023, you may file a complaint with the Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC) at ndpc.gov.ng. We encourage you to contact us first at popson0205@gmail.com so we can resolve the matter directly.

Both the admin dashboard and the main website share a localStorage key (pgs_sync) when opened in the same browser. In production, this will be replaced by a REST API — the admin writes to the database, the website reads from it.

Current sync events: Property approved → goes live on website · Property rejected → removed from website · User suspended → listings hidden · Dispute resolved → case closed

Production integration path: POST /api/admin/approve-property → webhook triggers website cache invalidation → listing appears within 30 seconds.
